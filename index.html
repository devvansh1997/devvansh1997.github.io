ECCV 2026 — Under Review

MaRIO: Multi-agent Collaborative Reasoning via Shared Observations in MLLMs

Sharma et al. — Center for Research in Computer Vision, UCF

Introduced MaRIO, a benchmark for evaluating how multimodal LLMs reason across multiple independent agent observations of a shared environment — without shared camera poses or geometric metadata. Evaluated 23 state-of-the-art MLLMs across 3,255 QA samples spanning 5 reasoning tasks: Distance Awareness, Egocentric Motion, Navigation, Relative Positioning, and Occlusion. All models fall substantially below human performance (73.3%), with the best model (Gemini-2.5-Pro) reaching 56.7%. Also explored geometry-guided synthetic view augmentation as a mitigation strategy.
